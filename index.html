REST IN PEACE HON. RAILA AMOLO ODINGA

Hon Raila Amolo Odinga was a prominent Kenyan politician and statesman who played a significant role in the country's political landscape. Born on January 7, 1945, he was the son of Jaramogi Oginga Odinga, Kenya's first Vice President. Raila Odinga was known for his advocacy for democracy, human rights, and social justice in Kenya.

A Life of Service

1945 - Born

Born in Maseno, Kisumu District, to Mary Juma Odinga and Jaramogi Oginga Odinga.

1970 - Return to Kenya

After studying in Germany, he returned to Kenya and established a business, the Standard Processing Equipment Construction & Erection Ltd.

1982 - Detained

He was detained without trial for six years by President Daniel arap Moi's government.

1992 - Member of Parliament

Elected as the Member of Parliament for Lang'ata, marking his official entry into elective politics.

2008 - Prime Minister

Became the second Prime Minister of Kenya, serving in a Grand Coalition Government.

2018 - The Handshake

His "Handshake" with President Uhuru Kenyatta symbolized a new era of political reconciliation and led to the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI).
